Ganesh Chaturthi

What is Ganesh Chaturthi?

Ganesh Chaturthi is a major Hindu festival that celebrates the birth of Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed deity revered as the god of wisdom, prosperity, and the remover of obstacles. Lord Ganesha is also considered the patron of arts and sciences and the deva (god) of beginnings. The festival is particularly popular in the Indian state of Maharashtra but is widely celebrated across India and in Hindu communities around the world.

When is it celebrated?

Ganesh Chaturthi is celebrated during the Hindu month of Bhadrapada, which typically falls between late August and early September in the Gregorian calendar. It lasts for 10 days. The timing of Ganesh Chaturthi is significant as it marks the end of the monsoon season in India. The festival is associated with the transition from the rainy season to the autumn harvest, symbolizing new beginnings

How is Ganesh Chaturthi celebrated?

At the start of the festival, idols of Ganesha are placed on raised platforms in homes or in elaborately decorated outdoor tents. Followed by shhodashopachara, or the 16 ways of paying tribute. Amid the chanting of Vedic hymns from religious texts like the Ganesh Upanishad, the idols are anointed with red sandalwood paste and yellow and red flowers.
